Getting started
6 questions
What is Folo and who is it for?
+

Folo is a personal finance tracker designed to give you a clear, honest picture of your money — without the complexity of professional accounting software or the limitations of a basic spreadsheet.

It's for anyone who wants to:

  • Know where their money goes each month
  • Set and stick to budgets across spending categories
  • Track balances across multiple accounts in one place
  • Build healthy financial habits over time

You don't need to be a finance expert. If you can log a transaction and set a monthly limit, you're already using Folo the right way.

How do I get started? Does it take long?
+

Setup takes about 5 minutes. Here's the recommended order:

  • Step 1 — Add your accounts. Go to the Accounts page and add each financial account you want to track: your bank account, savings, credit card, cash, etc. Enter the current balance for each.
  • Step 2 — Create your budgets. On the Budgets page, set a monthly spending limit for each category that matters to you (Food, Bills, Transport, etc.).
  • Step 3 — Start logging transactions. Add your income and expenses as they happen, or backfill the current month to get a complete picture immediately.
💡

Tip: Don't try to enter years of history on day one. Start with this month and build the habit. Historical data is most useful once you've been tracking for a few months.

Is Folo free to use?
+

Yes — Folo's core features are completely free. The free plan gives you access to:

  • Unlimited manual transactions
  • Up to 10 accounts
  • Full budgeting features
  • Dashboard, reports, and data export

We may introduce optional premium features in the future (such as automatic bank sync or advanced analytics), but we're committed to always maintaining a solid, genuinely useful free tier. We will never paywall features that are currently free.

Do I need to connect my bank account?
+

No — and this is a deliberate design choice. Folo is a manual-entry finance tracker. You record your own transactions by hand.

This approach has real advantages:

  • Privacy: we never have access to your bank login credentials or live account data
  • Awareness: manually logging expenses makes you more conscious of your spending habits
  • Control: you decide what to track, how to categorise it, and what to include
  • Reliability: no broken bank connections or missing transactions due to API failures

We may offer optional bank connectivity integrations in the future as a premium feature for users who prefer automatic import. Any such integration would be entirely opt-in.

What devices and platforms does Folo support?
+

Folo works on any modern device with a web browser. The app is fully responsive and works well on:

  • Desktop and laptop computers (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge)
  • Tablets (iPad, Android tablets)
  • Mobile phones (iPhone, Android — via browser)

Native iOS and Android apps are on our development roadmap. In the meantime, you can add Folo to your phone's home screen from your mobile browser for a near-native experience (look for "Add to Home Screen" in your browser menu).

Can I use Folo for business finances?
+

Folo is designed specifically for personal and household finance tracking. It is not intended for business accounting, VAT/tax reporting, invoicing, payroll, or multi-user business use.

If you're a freelancer, you may find Folo useful for tracking personal income and expenses, but for formal business accounting you should use dedicated tools like QuickBooks, Wave, or Xero.

For side projects or simple freelance income tracking, Folo works well as a supplement to (not a replacement for) proper business accounting records.

Accounts
5 questions
What types of accounts can I add?
+

You can add any type of financial account you want to track. Folo supports the following account types:

  • Checking — your everyday bank account
  • Savings — savings accounts, emergency funds, goal funds
  • Credit card — credit cards (tracked as negative balances by default)
  • Cash — physical cash on hand
  • Investment — brokerage, pension, or investment accounts

You can add as many accounts as you like and customise their name and colour for easy identification.

How do I keep account balances accurate?
+

Folo calculates your account balance by starting from the initial balance you set when creating the account and then adding or subtracting each transaction you record.

To keep balances accurate:

  • Set an accurate opening balance when you first create the account
  • Log transactions promptly — daily or weekly works best
  • Periodically reconcile with your actual bank statement

If your balance drifts due to forgotten transactions, use the Adjust Balance button on the Accounts page to manually correct it. This sets the balance directly without creating a transaction record.

📅

Tip: Set a weekly reminder to log transactions. Five minutes once a week is far easier than trying to reconstruct a month of spending from memory.

Why does my credit card show a negative balance?
+

Credit cards are liabilities — you owe that money to the card provider. In Folo, credit card balances are shown as negative numbers to reflect what you owe, consistent with standard accounting practice.

For example, if you owe $420.80 on your credit card, it will display as −$420.80. This is subtracted from your net worth calculation, giving you an accurate picture of your overall financial position.

When you make a payment to your credit card, log it as an expense from your checking account (category: Bills or a dedicated "Credit Card Payment" category). This reduces your checking balance and you should separately adjust your credit card balance or log the payment as income to the card account.

What happens to transactions if I delete an account?
+

When you delete an account, the account itself is removed from Folo, but the transactions associated with that account are not deleted — they remain in your transaction history and will show an unlinked account indicator.

If you want to fully remove all data associated with an account (both the account and its transactions), delete the transactions first from the Transactions page (filter by account), then delete the account.

How is net worth calculated?
+

Your net worth is the sum of all account balances — both positive (assets) and negative (liabilities).

Net Worth = Total Assets − Total Liabilities

For example: if you have $3,000 in your checking account, $8,000 in savings, and owe $400 on a credit card, your net worth is $3,000 + $8,000 − $400 = $10,600.

This calculation only reflects the accounts and balances you've manually entered in Folo. It is not a comprehensive view of your financial position and should not be used for financial planning without verification against your actual financial records.

Transactions
6 questions
How do I log income vs. an expense?
+

When adding a transaction, select the Type field:

  • Expense (−) — money leaving an account (groceries, bills, rent, etc.)
  • Income (+) — money coming into an account (salary, freelance payment, etc.)

The sign (+/−) is applied automatically based on your selection. You always enter the amount as a positive number — Folo handles the sign.

Expenses reduce your account balance; income increases it. Both types are reflected in your dashboard summary for the month.

What categories are available and can I customise them?
+

Folo includes the following built-in categories: Food, Bills, Entertainment, Health, Transport, Shopping, Housing, Savings, Income, and Other.

These cover the most common personal finance categories and are the same categories used by the budgeting system, so your spending automatically flows into your budget tracking.

Custom categories are on our development roadmap. In the meantime, you can use the Other category for anything that doesn't fit neatly into the existing list, and use the transaction description to add detail.

Can I edit or delete a transaction after saving it?
+

Yes, absolutely. Every transaction can be edited or deleted at any time.

  • On the Transactions page, each row has an edit (✏) and delete (🗑) button
  • Editing a transaction updates the associated account balance automatically
  • Deleting a transaction reverses its effect on the account balance
⚠️

Note: Deletion is permanent. There is no undo. If you make a mistake, you can always add the transaction back manually.

How do I record a transfer between my own accounts?
+

Transfers between your own accounts (e.g., moving money from checking to savings) require two transactions:

  • Transaction 1: Expense from the source account (e.g., −$500 from Checking, category: Savings)
  • Transaction 2: Income to the destination account (e.g., +$500 to Savings, category: Savings)

Use the Savings category for both so they don't inflate your expense totals in your monthly reports. This two-entry method keeps both account balances accurate.

A dedicated transfer feature that handles this in one step is planned for a future update.

Can I import transactions from a CSV or bank statement?
+

CSV import is on our development roadmap. Currently, transactions are entered manually.

For users wanting to backfill historical data, we recommend focusing on the current month first to establish the habit, then gradually adding history if needed. Most users find that 2–3 months of history is sufficient to see meaningful patterns.

If CSV import is a priority for you, let us know at hello@folo.app — user feedback directly shapes our development priorities.

How do I find old transactions quickly?
+

The Transactions page has several tools to help you find what you're looking for:

  • Search bar — searches transaction descriptions and categories in real time
  • Category filter — narrows to a single spending category
  • Account filter — shows only transactions from a specific account
  • Month filter — jump to any month in your history
  • Type toggle — switch between All, Income only, or Expenses only

All filters work together — for example, you can search for "Netflix" and filter to Entertainment to quickly find all entertainment subscriptions.

Budgets
5 questions
How do budgets work in Folo?
+

Budgets in Folo are monthly spending limits you set per category. Once set, Folo automatically tracks how much you've spent in that category during the current calendar month and shows your progress against the limit.

Each budget card shows:

  • How much you've spent vs. your limit
  • A progress bar that turns amber when you hit 80% and red when you exceed the limit
  • How much remains (or how much you're over)

Budgets reset automatically at the start of each calendar month.

What happens when I go over budget?
+

Going over budget doesn't lock anything or trigger penalties — it's purely informational. When you exceed a budget limit:

  • The progress bar turns red
  • The remaining amount shows as negative (e.g., "⚠ $42.50 over budget")
  • The dashboard overview will show the over-budget count

The goal is awareness, not restriction. Use over-budget notifications as a signal to review your spending and adjust either your habits or your budget limit for next month.

💡

Tip: If you consistently go over on a category, it may mean the budget is unrealistic rather than that your spending is wrong. Adjust the limit to something achievable and tighten it gradually over time.

Do unspent budgets roll over to the next month?
+

Currently, budgets do not roll over — each month starts fresh at zero spent against the same limit. This is the standard envelope-style budgeting approach.

Budget rollover (where unspent amounts carry forward) is a feature we're considering for a future update. If this is important to your budgeting approach, please let us know via the feedback form.

Can I set different budget limits for different months?
+

Not currently — each budget has a single fixed monthly limit that applies to all months. You can manually edit the limit at any time (changes take effect immediately for the current month's remaining calculations).

Variable monthly budgets (e.g., higher entertainment budget in December) are planned for a future update. For now, a workaround is to temporarily edit the limit at the start of months when you expect higher or lower spending in a category.

What's a realistic starting budget for common categories?
+

This varies widely depending on your location, income, and lifestyle, but a common starting framework for personal budgeting is the 50/30/20 rule:

  • 50% of after-tax income toward needs (Housing, Bills, Transport, Food basics)
  • 30% toward wants (Entertainment, Shopping, Dining out)
  • 20% toward savings and debt repayment

Start by tracking your actual spending for 2–4 weeks without a budget, then use those real numbers as your baseline. Setting budgets based on actual behaviour is more effective than guessing from scratch.

Note: this is general financial information, not personal financial advice. Consult a qualified financial advisor for guidance tailored to your situation.

Data & privacy
7 questions
Where is my data stored?
+

Folo stores your data in your browser's localStorage — meaning it lives entirely on your device and never leaves it. No data is sent to any server.

This means:

  • Your financial data stays 100% private on your own device
  • No account or internet connection is required for the app to function
  • Data is not synced between devices
  • Clearing your browser data will erase your Folo data
⬇️

Recommendation: Export your data regularly using the export feature as a backup. Store the exported file somewhere safe.

Do you sell my data or show ads?
+

No — categorically not. We do not sell, share, rent, or monetise your personal data in any way. We do not show ads in the application.

Your financial data is deeply personal. It tells a story about your life — where you live, what you eat, what you value, what you can and can't afford. We treat it with the seriousness that deserves.

Since Folo stores data locally on your device, we don't have access to it in the first place. We can't sell what we don't have.

How do I export my data?
+

You can export your transaction data as a CSV file at any time from the Transactions page. A full data export (including accounts and budgets) is available from the Accounts page under Settings.

We strongly recommend exporting your data regularly and storing a copy somewhere safe (cloud storage, email, external drive). Since data lives in your browser's localStorage, it can be lost if you clear browser data.

How do I delete all my data?
+

You can permanently reset all your Folo data from the Accounts page → Settings → Reset all data. This deletes everything stored in localStorage for Folo on that device — accounts, transactions, budgets, and settings.

This action is immediate and irreversible. There is no recovery after a reset. Make sure to export any data you want to keep before resetting.

Since data is stored locally, clearing your browser's site data or cache for folo.app will also remove all stored data.

Is my data safe if someone else uses my device?
+

Since Folo data is stored in your browser's localStorage, anyone with access to your browser on the same device can open Folo and see your financial data.

To protect your data:

  • Use a device with a screen lock or password
  • Use a private/incognito browser window when using Folo on a shared device (note: incognito data is deleted when the window closes)
  • Log out of the device or use a separate browser profile for your personal use

A PIN or password lock for the app itself is on our development roadmap.

What happens to my data if I switch browsers or devices?
+

localStorage is browser-specific and device-specific. Your data does not automatically sync to other browsers or devices.

To move your data to a new device or browser:

  • Export your data from the current device
  • Open Folo on the new device/browser
  • Import the exported file (import feature coming soon)

Cross-device sync via a cloud account is planned as a future feature for users who want it.

Where can I read your Privacy Policy and Terms?
+

Our full legal documents are available here:

  • Privacy Policy — how we handle your data, your rights, and our security practices
  • Terms and Conditions — the rules governing your use of Folo

Both documents are written to be readable by real people, not just lawyers. If anything is unclear, contact us at legal@folo.app.

Technical
5 questions
Why did my data disappear?
+

If your data has disappeared, the most common causes are:

  • Browser data cleared: clearing cookies, cache, or site data also clears localStorage
  • Different browser: data is browser-specific — data in Chrome won't appear in Firefox
  • Private/incognito mode: incognito sessions don't persist localStorage after the window closes
  • Different device: data doesn't sync between devices
  • Reset: you or someone with access to your browser may have pressed "Reset all data"

Unfortunately, if localStorage data is lost without a prior export, it cannot be recovered — this is a fundamental limitation of browser-based storage. This is why we strongly recommend exporting your data regularly.

Folo looks broken or isn't loading. What should I do?
+

Try these steps in order:

  • Hard refresh: press Ctrl+Shift+R (Windows/Linux) or Cmd+Shift+R (Mac) to force a full reload
  • Clear cache: clear your browser's cache and reload the page
  • Try another browser: test in a different browser to rule out browser-specific issues
  • Disable extensions: some browser extensions (especially ad blockers) can interfere with app functionality
  • Check the console: open your browser's developer tools (F12) and look for error messages in the Console tab

If none of these work, please contact us with details about your browser, operating system, and what you were doing when the issue occurred.

Does Folo work offline?
+

Yes — since all data is stored in your browser's localStorage, Folo works fully offline once the page has been loaded. You can add transactions, view your dashboard, and manage budgets without an internet connection.

You will need an internet connection to:

  • Load the app for the first time
  • Load any updates to the app
  • Use any future cloud sync features (when available)

For the best offline experience, add Folo to your device's home screen (look for "Add to Home Screen" in your browser menu).

How much data can localStorage hold?
+

Most modern browsers allocate 5–10 MB of localStorage space per website. In practical terms for Folo, this is enough for:

  • Thousands of transactions (each transaction is a few hundred bytes)
  • Dozens of accounts and budgets
  • Years of financial history for a typical user

You are very unlikely to hit this limit through normal use. If you do (which would require tens of thousands of transactions), Folo will display a warning and you can export and clear older data.

I found a bug. How do I report it?
+

We genuinely appreciate bug reports — they make Folo better for everyone. To report a bug:

  • Go to folo.app/contact and select "Bug report" as the topic
  • Include: what you were doing, what you expected to happen, what actually happened
  • Include your browser name and version (e.g., "Chrome 124 on Windows 11")
  • If possible, include a screenshot or copy any error messages from the browser console (F12 → Console)

We aim to acknowledge bug reports within 24 hours and fix critical issues as quickly as possible.
